This is a modified knuth's optimization range dp problem

we need to find mid where at mid, the left pile has close to equal sum as right pile.
This way, we know that any k (meaning if we split in the left pile) in left pile would result in us keeping the left pile since the right pile is larger.
Similarly, any k in right pile would result in us keeping right pile since left pile would be bigger.
Therefore, we can consciously choose which pile to keep even as Alice (force Bob). 

Additionally, we keep two lookup tables maxL and maxR, so that we can immediately look up the max value,
dependent on where mid is and which k we ultimately choose, instead of looking up current k and then manually recurse down to look for the total max value in said pile.

To update maxL and maxR, we only need the MAX of previously stored values in the pile range AND current total plus all past max choice scores stored in dp[i][j].

Same as knuth's optimization, we loop backwards for i and forward from i + 1 for j, because later on i will depend on i+1 and j will depend on j-1.

We also need a prefix sum array for fast stone value sum lookups.
